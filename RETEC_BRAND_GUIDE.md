## üí¨ WRITING GUIDELINES

### Do's ‚úÖ
- Use "you" and "your" (student-facing)
- Use "we" and "our" (when talking about RETEC)
- Mention Ghana/Ghanaian context naturally
- Lead with benefits, not features
- Use real career examples (Teacher, Nurse, Entrepreneur)
- Keep sentences short (max 20 words)
- Use active voice
- Include GHS currency when mentioning money

### Don'ts ‚ùå
- Don't say "disruptive" or "revolutionary"
- Don't oversell AI involvement
- Don't use edtech jargon
- Don't compare to other platforms
- Don't promise unrealistic outcomes
- Don't sound corporate or stiff
- Don't use American/British spellings inconsistently (pick one)

### Tone by Platform

**TikTok:**
- Conversational, energetic
- Short sentences
- Emoji-friendly (but not excessive)
- "We" and "you" language
- Example: "Want to be a nurse? ü©∫ Here are the exact JHS topics that matter most."

**Instagram:**
- Visual-first, inspiring
- Career stories, student wins
- Behind-the-scenes
- Example: "From Math class to Medical lab üî¨ See how these topics connect."

**WhatsApp/SMS:**
- Direct, respectful, helpful
- Quick value
- Clear next steps
- Example: "Hi! We map JHS subjects to careers. Which career interests you?"

**Email (Schools):**
- Professional, structured
- Lead with pain point
- Solution-focused
- Example: "Students often ask 'Why do I need this?' We help teachers answer that question."
